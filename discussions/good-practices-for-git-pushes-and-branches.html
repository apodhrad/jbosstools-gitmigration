let's say you cloned from repo1, branch master

then you put your changes on master

opens the pull request

while you were messing with it, they did more stuff on their master

later they integrate your change

for you to get the updates, you'll need to do a merge (or rebase) - something that you wouldn't need to do if you used a branch in the beginning

so, absolutely no burden on the receiving side, but harder for you to keep your master history identical to the "upstream" repo

TL;DR: branches are cheap, use them at will :)
